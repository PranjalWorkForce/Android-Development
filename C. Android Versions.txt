															# **ANDROID VERSIONS**

## **What is a Version?**

A **version** refers to a specific release of software that includes features, improvements, and bug fixes. Each new version typically enhances performance, security, and usability.

---

## **Android Versions Overview**

* The first commercial version, **Android 1.0**, was released on **September 23, 2008**.
* Early Android versions were named after **desserts (sweets)** in **alphabetical order**.

### **Dessert-Based Android Version Names**

Cupcake → Donut → Eclair → Froyo → Gingerbread → Honeycomb → Ice Cream Sandwich → Jelly Bean → KitKat → Lollipop → Marshmallow → Nougat → Oreo → Pie

**Note:**
Versions **A and B (Alpha & Beta)** were internal releases and not publicly named after desserts.

---

## **Android Versions and Features**

### **Cupcake (1.5)**

* Added basic Google apps (Search, Gmail, Contacts, Calendar)
* Widget support (e.g., search box on home screen)
* On-screen (virtual) keyboard

---

### **Donut (1.6)** – *September 15, 2009*

* Improved search (bookmarks & history included)
* Enhanced camera features
* Better app integration

---

### **Eclair (2.0–2.1)** – *October 2009*

* Camera improvements (zoom, flash, white balance)
* Support for SMS, MMS, and improved web browsing

---

### **Froyo (2.2–2.2.3)** – *May 20, 2010*

* Wi-Fi hotspot support
* Push notifications
* Bluetooth hands-free calling
* Improved performance and screen resolution support

---

### **Gingerbread (2.3–2.3.7)** – *December 6, 2010*

* Improved copy/paste functionality
* Front camera support
* NFC (Near Field Communication)
* Better audio support (AAC)

---

### **Honeycomb (3.0–3.2.6)** – *February 22, 2011*

* Designed specifically for tablets
* Introduced on Motorola Xoom
* Optimized for larger screens

---

### **Ice Cream Sandwich (4.0–4.0.4)** – *October 2011*

* Combined phone and tablet UI
* Introduced on Samsung Galaxy Nexus
* NFC-based data transfer (Android Beam)

---

### **Jelly Bean (4.1–4.3.1)** – *2012*

* Improved voice recognition (similar to Siri)
* Predictive text and gesture typing
* Smoother UI performance (Project Butter)

---

### **KitKat (4.4–4.4.4)** – *October 31, 2013*

* “OK Google” voice command
* Optimized for low-RAM devices

---

### **Lollipop (5.0–5.1.1)** – *2014*

* Introduced **Material Design**
* Improved notifications
* Separate work and personal profiles

---

### **Marshmallow (6.0–6.0.1)** – *2015*

* Runtime app permissions
* Fingerprint authentication
* Automatic data backup to Google Cloud
* Google Now from lock screen

---

### **Nougat (7.0–7.1.2)** – *2016*

* Multi-window support
* Emergency info on lock screen
* Improved settings menu

---

### **Oreo (8.0–8.1)** – *2017*

* Better battery management
* Background app limits
* Notification control improvements

---

### **Pie (9.0)** – *2018*

* Adaptive battery and brightness
* Gesture navigation
* Digital wellbeing features

---

## **Recent Android Versions (Number-Based Naming)**

Starting from Android 10, Google stopped using dessert names publicly.

* **Android 10** – *2019*
* **Android 11** – *September 8, 2020*
* **Android 12, 13, 14, 15 (latest versions)** – Continued improvements in privacy, UI, performance, and AI features

---

## **Summary**

* Android versions evolved from **basic functionality** to **AI-driven, secure, and highly customizable systems**.
* Naming changed from **dessert-based** to **numeric versions** after Android 9 (Pie).
